\paragraph{Question}
A shell is fired from a gun with initial velocity $\vec{v}_{shell}$, at an elevation $\theta$.
What value of $\theta$ should be chosen such that the shell hits an aircraft cruising at an altitude $h$,
directly overhead the gun when the shell is to be fired, and travelling at a constant velocity $\vec{v}_{plane}$?

\begin{center}

\paragraph{Solution}
Considering motion along the $x$-axis, clearly the velocity of the shell must equal the velocity of the
aircraft for their positions to coincide. Thus:
\begin{align*}
	\vec{v}_{plane}		\;=\;	\vec{v}_{shell}\cos\theta		\\\\
	\Aboxed{\theta		\;=\;	\cos^{-1}\left(\dfrac{\vec{v}_{plane}}{\vec{v}_{shell}}\right)}
\end{align*}
