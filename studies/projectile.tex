Consider the equations of motion of a projectile, launched at an elevation $\theta$ from a height $y_0$, experiencing uniform acceleration $-g$ along the $y$-axis.

\begin{align}
	x(t)	\;&=\;	v_0 \cos\theta									\\
	y(t)	\;&=\;	y_0 \;+\; v_0 \sin\theta \;-\;\frac{1}{2}gt^2
\end{align}

When the projectile hits the ground, we see that $y(t_{flight}) = 0$.

\begin{align*}
	0		\;&=\;	y_0 \;+\; v_0 \sin\theta \;-\;\frac{1}{2}gt^2	\\
	t		\;&=\;	\frac{1}{g}(\sin\theta \;+\; \sqrt{v_0^2\sin^2\theta + 2gy_0})
\end{align*}
